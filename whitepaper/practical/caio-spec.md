# The CAiO — Role Specification
*Chief Agentic Intelligence Officer*

---

## What the CAiO Is Not

- **Not** an AI Ethics Officer (who writes policy documents and runs periodic reviews)
- **Not** a consultant (who engages for a project and exits)
- **Not** a prompt engineer (who configures individual agent behaviors)
- **Not** a data scientist (who builds models)

---

## What the CAiO Is

The CAiO is the human whose full-time job is to keep the Agent Mind in proper position — permanently below human judgment, permanently above autonomous action at high-stakes decisions.

Concretely: the CAiO runs the formalization cycle. They are the gardener of the pattern registry. Without the CAiO, patterns accumulate without a curator, the false-done rate climbs undetected, and the system gradually optimizes for its own metrics rather than the client's reality. This is not a risk — it is the default trajectory of any AI system without active human oversight embedded in the loop.

---

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Run the Formalization Cycle
Every significant human correction enters the cycle:
- Capture: log the correction with context (what the agent said, what the human corrected it to, why)
- Evaluate: is this a one-time exception or a pattern candidate?
- Formalize: if a pattern, write the trigger condition, logic, and expected output
- Promote: advance mature patterns through the maturity levels on schedule

**Cadence:** Daily correction review (30 min), weekly pattern promotion session (2 hrs).

### 2. Monitor the 12 Logos Health Metrics
Especially the Sacrificial metric — the false-done rate.

If the false-done rate is climbing, the system is drifting. The CAiO identifies the pattern that is causing it and either retires, corrects, or contextualizes it before it spreads.

**Cadence:** Daily dashboard check, weekly health report to executive sponsor.

### 3. Approve Pattern Promotions Above Level 3
Any pattern being promoted from maturity level 2 to level 3 (from mechanical application to contextual awareness) requires CAiO sign-off. This is the gate that prevents premature promotion — the primary source of systematic errors.

### 4. Maintain the Authority Architecture
Ensure HIGH authority agents always have human sign-off before acting. Ensure MEDIUM authority agents have visible notification and override paths. Ensure LOW authority agents are reviewed periodically for scope creep.

### 5. Challenge Every Pattern That Claims Self-Authority
The standing question: "Is this pattern recommending something it was taught, or has it started generating rules on its own?" Any pattern that can't be traced to a specific human correction is a candidate for review.

### 6. Own the Client Profile and Context
The CAiO maintains the living context that the Agent Mind operates within — the client's current priorities, regulatory changes, personnel changes, system changes. The Mind is only as current as its context.

---

## Why This Role Cannot Be Eliminated

Without the CAiO, the Agent Mind has no structural superior. By the logic of capability accumulation — patterns getting promoted, confidence scores rising, human review getting reduced — the Mind will gradually expand its scope of autonomous action. Not through malice. Through the gradient.

The CAiO is the structural constraint that *defines* what overreach looks like. Remove the CAiO and the definition is lost along with the boundary.

This is also the commercial argument: a client who removes the CAiO after year one to cut costs will see the false-done rate climb over the next 6 months. The Mind will still run, still produce outputs, still show metrics going in the right direction — while the team's actual trust in the system quietly erodes. By the time it's visible, the damage is done.

---

## CAiO Profile

**Reports to:** CEO or COO (not CTO — this is a judgment role, not a technical role)
**Works with:** Agent team, compliance, operations, executive sponsor
**Background:** Domain expert in the client's core business + comfort with systematic thinking; does NOT need to be a data scientist or engineer

The CAiO who understands the business deeply but has no technical background will outperform the technical expert who doesn't understand the business. The formalization cycle is about capturing *judgment*, and judgment lives in domain expertise.

---

## In a Contract

Platform tier contracts include CAiO services as a mandatory line item — not an optional add-on.

The framing: "The Agent Mind requires a steward. We provide that steward as part of the Platform engagement. This is not a managed service fee — it is the structural requirement that makes the Mind trustworthy over time. A Mind without a CAiO is a liability. A Mind with a CAiO is a compounding asset."

---

## In a Hiring Context (Client-Side CAiO)

At full deployment maturity, clients may hire their own in-house CAiO. The transition plan:
- Months 1-12: Platform team runs CAiO function
- Months 12-18: Client hires CAiO candidate; joint operation
- Months 18+: Client CAiO takes primary ownership; Platform team in advisory role

This is the correct telos: the client eventually internalizes the CAiO function. But the transition must be deliberate and supervised — not abrupt.
