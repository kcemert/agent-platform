# The Seven-Layer Stack
*Product architecture and pricing logic*

---

## The Stack

```
HUMAN CONSCIOUSNESS  ← permanently above; the one who sees and corrects
      ↕
AGENT MIND           ← singular, cumulative, grows through correction
      ↕
WORKER BEE AGENTS    ← many, task-specific, replaceable
      ↕
AGENT PLATFORM       ← infrastructure: scheduling, retry, audit, HITL
      ↕
TOOLS                ← discrete, stateless, callable functions
      ↕
DATA / THE WORLD     ← structured + unstructured substrate
```

---

## The Critical Distinction

Most AI vendors sell layers 1-4 (data, tools, agents, platform). That is the commodity layer. It is commoditizing fast — models get cheaper, APIs get simpler, frameworks get easier.

**Layer 5 (Agent Mind) is the non-commodity.** It cannot be purchased, copied, or spun up in a weekend. It must be grown through the formalization cycle — months of human correction being captured, validated, and internalized. The competitor who starts building their client's Agent Mind next year will be 12 months behind. The Mind is the moat.

---

## Layer Breakdown

### Layer 1: Data
What it is: the substrate — ERP records, sensor data, emails, documents, real-time feeds.
Who builds it: client's existing systems.
Agent platform role: connect to it, read it, write back when authorized.

### Layer 2: Tools
What it is: 38+ discrete callable functions — each takes input, produces output, remembers nothing.
Who builds it: the platform team.
Client value: the building blocks that reduce time-to-deploy for new agents.

### Layer 3: Worker Bee Agents
What it is: chains tools together with logic — perceive, reason, act.
Who builds it: the platform team (from blueprints).
Key property: **mortal and replaceable**. The agent that runs the inventory check today can be swapped for a better one next month without losing anything the Mind has learned.

### Layer 4: Agent Platform
What it is: the infrastructure that makes agents reliable — scheduling, retry logic, HITL gates, health monitoring, audit trail.
Who builds it: the platform team.
Client value: agents that are observable, governable, and auditable. This is what separates a demo from a production deployment.

### Layer 5: Agent Mind
What it is: the accumulated, formalized wisdom that governs *how* agents reason — not just what they do. The pattern registry. The maturity model. The correction history. The context.
Who builds it: **grows through the formalization cycle across months of real use.**
Key property: **singular and irreplaceable**. You cannot rebuild the Mind by redeploying agents. The Mind IS the correction history — the accumulated record of human judgment tested against reality.

## Pricing Logic

| Layer | What you're buying | Pricing model |
|---|---|---|
| Workers + Tools | Replaceable modules | Per-agent subscription |
| Platform | The infrastructure | Infrastructure subscription |
| Agent Mind | A living compounding asset | **Equity + long-term revenue share** |

The Mind is priced like equity because it IS equity. It grows in value over time. It is irreplaceable without rebuilding. It is the senior employee who carries institutional knowledge — not the contractor who can be re-hired.

This means: clients who engage at the Platform tier are not buying a software product. They are partnering in the growth of an asset that will be worth more in year 3 than in year 1, and more in year 5 than in year 3.

"There are three things we're building together. First, the agents — specific tools for specific jobs, like automating your inventory replenishment or flagging compliance exceptions. Those are subscription line items; we swap them out as needs change. Second, the platform — the infrastructure that makes the agents reliable, auditable, and governable. That's a different subscription tier.

Third — and this is the part most AI vendors don't offer — is the Agent Mind. That's the accumulated judgment of your team, formalized and embedded in the system over time. Every correction your team makes teaches the system something. After 12 months, the Mind knows your business. After 3 years, it's irreplaceable — it carries institutional knowledge that would take months to reconstruct if lost. That's why we price it differently: not a subscription, but a partnership with skin in the game on both sides."
