Platform Demo — Kimre Inc.

A guided walkthrough of your agent platform. Each stop takes 2–5 minutes.

💡 Tip: open each tool in a new tab to keep your place.
01

How much is mist carryover costing you?

Start by quantifying the problem. The Business Case Calculator takes your operating conditions and estimates the annual cost of mist carryover in your process — and the savings a Kimre mist eliminator would deliver. Use the Sulfuric Acid Absorber scenario to see a live example.

What to do
  1. 1 Click "Open Calculator" below to launch the tool in a new tab.
  2. 2 Select "Sulfuric Acid (H₂SO₄)" from the Application dropdown.
  3. 3 Enter Gas Flow Rate: 4500 Nm³/hr, Inlet Mist Loading: 850 mg/m³.
  4. 4 Review the Annual Savings and Payback Period on the right panel.
What you're seeing

The calculator uses Kimre's application database to auto-fill chemical benchmarks, product recommendations, and efficiency targets. This is the same logic the RFQ-to-Quote Agent uses to size equipment — shown here as a self-service tool for plant engineers.

Open Business Case Calculator → RFQ-to-Quote Agent logic (demo mode)
02

30 seconds from application to spec sheet

This is the tool that changes the quote process. An applications engineer enters operating conditions, and the generator produces a formal Equipment Specification Sheet — product selected, material confirmed, vessel sized, performance guaranteed. What used to take 2 hours now takes 30 seconds to generate and 15 minutes to review.

What to do
  1. 1 Click "Open Spec Generator" below to launch the tool.
  2. 2 Click the orange "Load Demo" button in the top-right corner.
  3. 3 Watch the spec generate automatically for the Mosaic Fertilizer H₂SO₄ scenario.
  4. 4 Review the output: product selection (B-GON® Mist Eliminator), material (CPVC/PP), sizing, and performance guarantee.
  5. 5 Note the confidence flags — amber = review recommended, red = clarification needed.
What you're seeing

The generator applies Kimre's engineering logic: chemical compatibility rules, sizing equations, standard nozzle sizing, and material selection. The output is a print-ready spec sheet that can be issued with a quote.

Open Spec Generator → Spec Generator (internal engineering tool)
03

Sam's production queue, live

This is Sam Rivera's (Engineering Lead) daily command center. It shows every active fabrication order, material stock levels, shortages, and agent recommendations — all in one view. The Material Requisition Agent is the live pilot: it scans open orders against material stock and creates purchase requisitions.

What to do
  1. 1 Click "Open Engineering Dashboard" below.
  2. 2 Review the KPI strip: orders pending BOM, in fabrication, material alerts.
  3. 3 Find the "Material Shortage" widget — click "Approve PR" on one item.
  4. 4 Click "▶ Run Material Requisition Agent" (top-right run button).
  5. 5 Watch the agent steps animate and recommendations appear in the queue.
What you're seeing

The agent runs in seconds and surfaces exactly what needs a decision. Sam approves or modifies each recommendation. No email chains, no spreadsheet lookups — every action is logged automatically.

Open Engineering Dashboard → Material Requisition Agent — Live Pilot
04

Mary's view of the entire business

This is what Mary Gaston (President) sees. One page covering: the RFQ pipeline value, active order backlog, on-time delivery rate, agent program ROI, and any decisions waiting for her sign-off. The goal: understand the full state of the business in under 5 minutes.

What to do
  1. 1 Click "Open Executive Dashboard" below.
  2. 2 Review the Quote-to-Cash funnel (6 stages from RFQ to Shipped).
  3. 3 Check the Agent Program Scorecard — hours saved, cumulative value recovered, payback progress.
  4. 4 Review the Escalation Queue — any decisions routed to Mary for approval.
What you're seeing

Every agent run that exceeds a threshold routes to Mary for approval. She sees the business in aggregate, not in her inbox. The agent ROI scorecard answers the question every executive asks: "Is this worth the investment?"
