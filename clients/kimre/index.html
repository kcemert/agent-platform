Why ETO changes everything for agent design

Most agent blueprints are built for Make-to-Stock (MTS) manufacturers — companies with standard SKUs, predictable demand, and replenishment cycles. Kimre is the opposite.

In Engineer-to-Order, no two products are identical. Every order triggers a custom design, a one-off bill of materials, and specialty material procurement. There are no stock replenishment cycles. Demand cannot be forecast in the traditional sense.

This fundamentally shifts which agents are high-value for Kimre:

Agent Type ETO Value Why
RFQ-to-Quote Very High Days to quote manually; agent reduces to hours
Material Requisition Very High Each order triggers unique specialty purchases
Quality Compliance High EPA spec must be validated pre-fab, not post-ship
Order Milestone Notifier High ETO customers wait weeks; proactive comms reduce inbound
Standard Replenishment Low No standard SKUs — replenishment model doesn’t apply
Demand Forecast Low Project-based lumpy demand; statistical forecast unreliable
