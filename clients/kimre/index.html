Strategic Opportunities
4 Prioritized Strategic Opportunities
1
Installed base mapping → proactive retrofit revenue. Kimre has shipped hundreds of units over 50 years. Systematically cataloguing the installed base turns a passive asset into an active sales pipeline. Every unit is a future retrofit RFQ.
2
EPC specification relationships → Bechtel/Fluor design standards. If Kimre’s B-GON® technology is specified in an EPC firm’s standard equipment library, it flows through into every project that firm designs — without Kimre needing to win each bid independently.
3
Geographic expansion signals → new African/Asian H₂SO₄ capacity. New sulfuric acid plants coming online in Africa and Southeast Asia represent greenfield mist eliminator demand. Research Agent can surface these signals before RFQs are issued.
4
Digital tools as competitive moat. Offering customers a spec generator, an asset dashboard, and proactive service communications creates switching costs that a competitor cannot easily replicate with product alone.

Current Pain Points

Non-Standard Customer Specs

Customer specs arrive in mixed IPS/metric, with non-standard nozzle sizes or custom corrosion requirements. Each must be manually translated.

Manual BOM in Excel

BOM creation is manual Excel work — error-prone, time-consuming, and requires re-work when specs change late in the cycle.

Changes via Email

Engineering changes tracked via email chain. No formal change log — risk of fabrication starting against outdated specs.

ETO vs MTS: ETO orders require full BOM creation per order. MTO/MTS Fiber Bed Cage orders use pre-built BOM templates — Sam pulls from library rather than creates from scratch. Both lanes tracked in the fabrication queue. Agent handles ETO lane; template library handles MTS.

Retrofit Lifecycle & Account Health

The strategic loop: Every unit Kimre installs is a future retrofit opportunity. Plants last 20–30 years. A mist eliminator typically needs replacement or refurbishment every 5–15 years. If Kimre tracks the installed base systematically, they own the account forever — if they don’t, Koch gets the retrofit RFQ.
Retrofit Reorder Agent
How It Works

Scans accounts by last order date vs. typical reorder interval for the product type purchased. Flags accounts approaching or past expected retrofit window. Drafts proactive outreach referencing the specific product purchased, the installation date, and the expected service life — queues for sales review before sending.
